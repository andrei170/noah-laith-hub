Key Design Decisions

Why a cohort, not a course: A course is asynchronous, lower-perceived-value, and requires content production before any revenue. A cohort is synchronous, premium, and fills before a single session is recorded. Noah's time cost is a weekly call. The cohort framework does all the heavy lifting.

Why a quiz, not a lead magnet: A quiz qualifies. A lead magnet attracts. Noah's audience is not discovery-stage - they are already engaged with the brand. A quiz segments them by readiness and routes each subscriber to the correct tier. A generic lead magnet does not do this.

Why a unified hub, not a redesigned TLC site: The TLC site serves existing patients and clinical inquiries. Changing it carries risk and is outside AG1's scope. The hub is a separate, lightweight surface that serves the @thestoicbiohacker audience specifically and does not affect the existing clinical front-end.

Why telemedicine-forward framing: Noah's four-city operation is a major competitive advantage. Positioning TLC as a global precision medicine network (not a local Miami clinic) serves all four geographies from one digital surface and removes geography as a barrier for international high-income clients.
