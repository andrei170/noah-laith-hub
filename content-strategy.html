Noah Laith | Content Strategy (30-Day)

Prospect: Noah Laith (@thestoicbiohacker, Founder + Clinical Director, The Longevity Clinic) Register: Stoic / biohacker / executive-elite / precision-clinical Purpose: 30-day content angle plan for @thestoicbiohacker and TLC digital surfaces Verified: 2026-05-16

Governing Principles

  • Stoic register: Every piece of content is grounded in a stoic principle or precision performance concept. Not wellness. Not inspiration. Discipline, sovereignty, systems.
  • ICP-calibrated: Written for C-suite executives, elite athletes, high-performance entrepreneurs. Not for general fitness audiences.
  • Clinical specificity: Reference real protocols, real compounds, real mechanisms. Vague wellness language is the competition. Precision is the differentiator.
  • Distribution without sales: Content builds authority and captures email. It does not sell directly. The quiz and cohort do the selling.
  • Regulated-niche compliance: No cure claims, no guarantee language, no outcome promises. All clinical references use hedged framing ("as documented in clinical literature," "consistent with published research on").

Angle: Stoic philosophy (Memento Mori, Premeditatio Malorum) applied to recovery, stress tolerance, and performance under pressure. The body as a system, not a problem to solve. CTA: Link in bio - Longevity Audit quiz (no explicit mention, just "assess your baseline").

Day 2 (Wednesday) - Long-form caption / LinkedIn post Title: "Why discipline without precision is just suffering with extra steps" Angle: Executive-elite critique of hustle culture. Precision protocols (sleep architecture, peptide timing, hormonal baseline) outperform effort-based approaches. Discipline is a design problem, not a willpower problem.

Day 3 (Friday) - Educational carousel or static post Title: "The three-tier performance architecture" Angle: How top performers structure their recovery, cognition, and longevity - the same architecture used in TLC's clinical programmes. Visual: three tiers (recovery baseline / peak performance layer / longevity extension).

Day 4 (Saturday) - Story series Title: "Ask me anything on stoic biohacking" Format: Q+A story series. Noah answers 5-7 questions from followers. Builds parasocial depth.

Platform-Specific Notes

Instagram (@thestoicbiohacker): - Reels perform best. Aim for 60-90 second educational reels over talking-head formats. - Carousel posts for frameworks and multi-step concepts. - Story Q+A series twice per month (high engagement, low production cost). - Link in bio routes to quiz. Always.

LinkedIn: - Long-form article format preferred over short posts for this audience. - Executive register required. No casual language. - B2B angle: decision-makers evaluating TLC for their teams or for their own executive health programme.

YouTube ("The Stoic Biohacker"): - Long-form only (8-20 minutes). Not Shorts. - Deep dives on peptide protocols, longevity architecture, stoic philosophy applied to performance. - Each video generates Reel clips for Instagram (5-7 clips per long-form video).

Compliance Layer (applies to all content)

  • No cure claims, no outcome guarantees, no diagnostic language.
  • "Consult a qualified medical practitioner" appended to any content referencing specific protocols.
  • "Results vary by individual" on any before/after or outcome-adjacent content.
  • TLC delivers clinical services. Content is educational. AG1 builds the front-end. These roles do not overlap.
