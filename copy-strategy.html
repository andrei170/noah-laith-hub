Tone Calibration

Do: Direct statements. Short sentences. Clinical specificity. Data points. Named programmes. Concrete timelines.

Do not: Inspirational filler. Vague wellness language. Hype phrases. Long preamble before the point.

Format: Minimal punctuation for effect. No em dashes. Parenthetical asides used sparingly. Bold for emphasis on specific credentials or numbers, not for decoration.

Sentence rhythm: Mix short declarative statements with longer explanatory sentences. Noah's own copy does this - "Excellence is the target" is five words. The paragraph that follows is dense. Mirror that rhythm.

Compliance Layer (mandatory for all Noah Laith copy)

All copy that references clinical outcomes, TLC services, or health benefits must carry the following framing:

  • AG1 builds the marketing funnel. The Longevity Clinic delivers all clinical services.
  • Never attribute clinical outcomes to AG1.
  • TLC outcome language (e.g., "full recovery in three months") is sourced from TLC's own published founder bio - this is their claim, not AG1's claim. Attribute it as such: "As documented in Noah's founder story..." or "As described on thelongevityclinic.net..."
  • No yield claims, cure claims, or outcome guarantees on any AG1-built surface.
  • Full medical/FTC disclaimer in footer on every page.
